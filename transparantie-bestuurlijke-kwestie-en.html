⚖️ Your Rights as a Member

Art. 9:3 WVV

You have the right to inspect all books and documents of the association. Denial is only allowed in exceptional circumstances justified in writing.

Art. 33 WVV

The board must follow proper procedures. Arbitrary decisions violate your rights. Members can demand adherence to statutory procedures.

Art. 2:97 WVV

The board is responsible for good management. Failures that harm members can result in personal liability for board members.

Art. 2:56 WVV

Board members are personally liable for violations. Unlawful decisions affecting members can trigger personal responsibility.

✅ Recommendations for Members

1. Know Your Rights

Study the WVV (Legal Structure Act). Your rights are clearly defined by Belgian law, regardless of internal policies.

2. Document Everything

Keep copies of payments, emails, receipts, and all correspondence. This creates an audit trail.

3. Request Access Formally

Submit written requests for documents with legal references (Art. 9:3 WVV). Require written responses.

4. Escalate if Denied

If access is refused, send a formal legal notice (sommatie). This creates legal documentation of the violation.

5. Seek Professional Legal Advice

If disputes persist, consult a lawyer specializing in association law. Your rights are enforceable.
